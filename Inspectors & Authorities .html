3(a) After conducting the inspection the ALO has to submit a report in the prescribed profoma shown in
Annexure – II to the ACL within two days from the date of inspection
along with the proposals on the followup action to be taken against the employer.
ACL shall examine the proposals and issue necessary
approval to the ALO within 7 days to take up prosecution of filing claims or issue warning or drop action
according to the merits of each case. At any stage, the DCL also may direct the
ACL to forward such proposals of the ALOs to him for examining the matter and issue necessary
instructions to the ALO on the follow up action through the ACL.
(b) LOs and ACLs have to submit copy of the inspection report and the proposals on the follow up action
to the concerned DCL within two days after conducting inspections. After examining the proposals,
the DCL shall issue necessary approvals to LOs/ACLs within 7 days to file claims or to take up prosecutions
or to issue warning or to drop action according to the merits of each case.
4(a) The complaints received by various officers shall be submitted to the concerened DCLs and he shall
examine them objectively and if it is necessary, he will authorise any of the subordinate officers
to conuct inspections on such establishments and submit report an after receing the report, the DCL shall examine
the issue necessary instructions for taking further action against that particular undertakings. Inspectors under Child Labour Appointment of Inspectors and fixation of their local limits under certain Labour Legislations (additional Inspectors need to be appointed under the Child Labour (Prohibition & Regulation) Act, 1986 in implementation of elimination of Child Labour. Mid day meals Scheme is also being implemented in the State and need for greater involvement of the State Govt in identification and rehabilitation of Child Labour. GO Ms No 98 of LET&F (Lab.IV) Dept dated 08.11.2010. Ref:- 1. G.O.Ms.No.37, Labour, Employment, Training & Factories (Labour.IV) Department, dated 6th June, 2008.
2. From Commissioner of Labour, Lr.No.E1/12195/2005, dated: 30.11.09.

From Commissioner of Labour, Lr.No.N2/12195/2005, dated: 7.05.10		 The Government noticed that the issue of Child Labour is assuming importance because there is an effort in certain International Quarters to link Child Labour with trade. International Labour Organization has adopted a Global Report to eliminate Child Labour in the worst condition by 2016. Mid-day Meals Scheme is also being implemented in the State in the National Child Labour Projects. The need for greater involvement of the State Government in identification and rehabilitation of Child Labour has also been felt by the Central Monitoring Committee on Child Labour. It is therefore felt that additional Inspectors need to be appointed under the Child Labour (Prohibition & Regulation) Act, 1986 in implementation of elimination of Child Labour. Accordingly, in supersession of the notification issued in the G.O.Ms.No.41, Labour, Employment, Training & Factories (Labour.IV) Department, dated 20th May, 2010, the following notification shall be published in an extraordinary issue of the A.P. Gazette, dated20th May 2010
• Section 17 of the Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986 (Act 61 of 1986),
• Section 57 of the Andhra Pradesh Shops and Establishments Act, 1988 (Andhra Pradesh Act No.20 of 1988),
• sub-section (1) of Section 4 of the Motor Transport Workers Act, 1961 (Act 27 of 1961),
